Black Knights

Painting skeletons is always fun and satisfying, and these guys had some nice extra details like vines and leaves. I probably used too many subassemblies painting them, but it made painting the horse cloaks easier, even if everything else was harder. Also, I tried to paint on the scratched edges of the armour manually with a fine brush to try and up my skills, but I just couldn't get it to look good at all. I switched back to drybrushing and it worked way better, so even though it's so easy it feels like cheating, I'm probably going to keep drybrushing scratches and damage for a long time.

Paints Used:

  • Bone: Zandri Dust, Seraphim Sepia all over, drybrush/highlight Ushabti Bone, highlight Screaming Skull
  • Fabric: Abaddon Black, heavy drybrush Eshin Grey and Dawnstone
  • Armour: Abaddon Black, drybrush Leadbelcher, highlight Stormhost Silver
  • Metal: Leadbelcher, Nuln Oil all over, highlight Stormhost Silver
  • Old Brass: Balthasar Gold, Nihilakh Oxide all over, highlight Balthasar Gold and Sycorax Bronze
  • Rotting Flesh: Rakarth Flesh, Reikland Fleshshade all over, highlight Flayed One Flesh
  • Vines: Dryad Bark, Agrax Earthshade all over, highlight Gorthor Brown
  • Leaves: Waaagh Flesh, Biel-tan Green all over, highlight Warboss Green and Skarsnik Green
  • Sword Gem: Vallejo White, Druchii Violet all over, Ardcoat
  • Base: Vallejo Brown Earth Texture, Agrax Earthshade all over, drybrush Screaming Skull, Gamers Grass Dry Tufts and TinyTufts Beige, Steel Legion Drab rim
